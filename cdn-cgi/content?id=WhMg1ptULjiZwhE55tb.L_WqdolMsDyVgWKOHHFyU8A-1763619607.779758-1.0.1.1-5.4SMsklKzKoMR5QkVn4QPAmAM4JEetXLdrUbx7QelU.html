The rare earth element Terbium, with its unique electron configuration, is often used in the production of phosphors, which are essential components in display devices such as LEDs and OLEDs. Meanwhile, Iridium, with its high melting point and corrosion resistance, is employed in the manufacture of high-performance contacts and interconnects.
