Space42 Showcases AI-Powered Geospatial Intelligence at GITEX GLOBAL


Space42 the leading UAE-based AI-powered SpaceTech enterprise offering satellite operations and AI-driven geospatial solutions for governments and businesses worldwide, is one of the headline exhibitors at GITEX GLOBAL 2025. Christian Andersen, Senior Vice President of Space42, talked to our Press Office about how the company is building AI technologies for geospatial intelligence through AIoT, AI Discovery Engine, and Physical AI.

How are Physical AI and AIoT influencing the evolution of various industry sectors and societies globally?

Physical AI and AIoT are driving fundamental transformation across industries and societies globally, shifting from experimental technologies to operational infrastructure that reshapes how organizations function.

Three developments explain their growing influence.

Physical AI has reached an operational threshold. Systems that once existed as prototypes now perform reliably in field conditions. Robotics, unmanned platforms, and edge-AI operate actively in logistics, manufacturing, and security where speed, autonomy, and resilience are essential. Rather than displacing human input, these systems augment it at scale.

AIoT has made intelligence ambient. AI is now embedded within infrastructure rather than merely interfacing with it. By fusing geospatial data from satellites with terrestrial sensor networks, AIoT platforms generate real-time situational awareness. This transformation is most visible in how cities monitor mobility flows, energy usage, and environmental stress.

The diffusion of these technologies is accelerating across income levels and regions. Their impact appears in urban planning, asset management, and public service delivery. They are no longer future-facing concepts but foundational layers in today's digital economies.

This shift underpins both Space42's mission and business model. Our geospatial analytics platform GIQ integrates AIoT data streams to inform decision-making in disaster response and infrastructure monitoring. Our AI Discovery Engine (AID) brings Physical AI into operational domains including logistics and agriculture. These are deployed systems that contribute daily to how institutions plan, act, and adapt, creating smarter and more responsive operating environments in the UAE and globally.

Why are AI technologies increasingly vital for safeguarding national sovereignty and boosting economic competitiveness?

AI technologies are foundational to national sovereignty and future economic leadership, determining which nations control critical systems and shape global competition.

Three functions illustrate AI's strategic importance.

1. AI acts as the command layer of critical systems. It optimizes satellite constellations, automates situational awareness, and delivers early-warning signals across sectors that underpin national resilience, from defense logistics to environmental surveillance. These capabilities define how nations maintain autonomy in complex, high-stakes environments.

2. AI expands economic potential by enabling scale without compromise. It compresses decision cycles, streamlines operations, and transforms raw data into actionable intelligence. For national planning, this means better resource allocation, faster policy execution, and broader access to advanced services.

3. AI influences global rules of engagement. Nations that master AI do more than deploy it—they shape the standards, ethical norms, and cross-border protocols governing its future use.

Space42 was founded with this understanding. AI is not treated as an overlay but engineered into the core of our satellite communications and geospatial analytics platforms. Our architecture serves dual-use requirements from inception, delivering sovereign capabilities that scale commercially and commercial innovations that reinforce sovereign control.

This model demonstrates that sovereignty and competitiveness are not opposing forces but mutually reinforcing outcomes of the same AI-powered system.

What role should governments and regulators play in building a secure, robust AI ecosystem that drives progress for businesses and individuals?

Governments and regulators must balance acceleration with assurance to build secure, robust AI ecosystems that drive progress for businesses and individuals.

Governments must act as anchor customers. In early-stage AI markets, public demand can reduce innovation risks and attract private capital. This model has proven effective in space-based services and applies equally to AI development.

Regulators must provide guardrails without curtailing experimentation. The objective is ensuring safe AI deployment rather than constraining innovation. This requires agile regulatory frameworks, open architectures, and alignment on ethical standards.

Policy must promote sovereign capability. This means investing in national data infrastructure, computing capacity, and skills development. Without these foundational elements, no country can achieve genuine digital sovereignty. 

Could you elaborate on the significance of participating in GITEX GLOBAL for your organisation, and what aspects of the event most interest or excite you?

GITEX GLOBAL serves as a strategic platform for dialogue, discovery, and deployment, connecting Space42 with the decision-makers and innovators who drive transformation across critical sectors.

The significance of GITEX lies in its ability to bring together decision-makers, innovators, and operators across sectors central to our mission. For Space42, it provides a forum to engage with existing partners and explore new collaborations, particularly in domains where space-based insights, secure communications, and artificial intelligence drive operational value.

GITEX offers an opportunity to showcase the maturity of our platforms, especially in geospatial analytics and satellite connectivity, while helping us understand how these capabilities integrate into the priorities of business, government, and infrastructure stakeholders. The quality of conversations excites us most. The event connects us with those who have mandates to act and the willingness to adopt solutions that are both technically sound and strategically aligned.

GITEX represents relevance, demonstrating how our technologies support transformation agendas in real-world contexts where space-based capabilities can deliver measurable impact.

ABOUT THE COMPANY:

 Space42 (ADX: SPACE42) is a UAE-based AI-powered SpaceTech company that integrates satellite communications, geospatial analytics and artificial intelligence capabilities to enlighten the Earth from space. Formed in 2024 by the successful merger of Bayanat and Yahsat, Space42’s global reach allows it to address the rapidly evolving needs of its customers in governments, enterprises, and communities. Space42 comprises two business units: Space Services and Smart Solutions. Space Services focuses on upstream satellite operations for both fixed and mobile satellite services. Smart Solutions integrates geospatial data acquisition and processing with AI to inform decision-making, enhance situational awareness, and improve operational efficiency. Major shareholders include G42, Mubadala, and IHC.
