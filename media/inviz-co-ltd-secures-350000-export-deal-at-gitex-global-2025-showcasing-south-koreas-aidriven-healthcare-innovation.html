INVIZ Co., Ltd. Secures $350,000 Export Deal at GITEX GLOBAL 2025, Showcasing South Korea's AI-Driven Healthcare Innovation


Korean AI Healthcare Company Signs Contract with Indonesian Government Partner, Demonstrating Global Competitiveness of Government-Backed Digital Health Solutions

DUBAI, UAE – October 16, 2025 –  INVIZ Co., Ltd., a leading South Korean AI healthcare company, has secured a $350,000 export contract with Telenasindo, an official partner of Indonesia's Ministry of Health, at GITEX GLOBAL 2025, the Middle East's largest ICT exhibition held in Dubai from October 13-17.

The landmark deal, featuring Inviz's flagship AI Medimage Care platform and Cloud Care Life PACS system, marks a significant milestone in the global expansion of South Korea's government-supported AI healthcare initiatives. The achievement underscores the growing international demand for innovative digital health solutions developed through public-private partnerships.

Government-Backed Innovation Driving Global Healthcare Transformation

The AI Medimage Care platform is a direct outcome of the "Inter-Ministerial Cooperation-Based AI Expansion Project," a strategic initiative led by South Korea's Ministry of Science and ICT (MSIT) and the National IT Industry Promotion Agency (NIPA). This program exemplifies how government collaboration with private sector innovation can create globally competitive solutions that address real-world healthcare challenges.

"This platform represents more than technological advancement—it's a model for how government data resources combined with private sector AI expertise can solve critical healthcare challenges," said Sung-Chul Park, CEO of Inviz Inc. "We've created a system that standardizes medical imaging quality assurance from hospital examination requests through regulatory agency evaluations to integrated Ministry of Health monitoring."

The AI Medimage Care platform addresses a critical gap in healthcare systems worldwide: the lack of standardized processes for medical imaging quality control. By integrating examination workflows across hospitals, quality inspection agencies, and government health authorities, the platform establishes a national-level quality management framework that has garnered significant interest from health officials across multiple countries.

Advanced Technology Meeting Market Demand

At GITEX GLOBAL 2025, where over 6,000 companies from 170+ countries participated, Inviz showcased its cloud-based medical imaging platform alongside various digital healthcare technologies at the Korea Pavilion organized by NIPA. The company's exhibition attracted substantial attention from international buyers seeking cutting-edge healthcare solutions.

The export contract includes two key solutions:

  1. AI Medimage Care: A comprehensive platform enabling integrated management of the entire medical imaging quality assurance workflow—from hospital examination requests to quality inspection agency assessments and Ministry of Health unified monitoring. The system standardizes previously fragmented examination processes across medical institutions and establishes a national quality control framework.
  2. Cloud Care Life: An ISO 13485 and US FDA-certified web-based PACS (Picture Archiving and Communication System) for medical image storage and transmission, designed to support digital transformation in healthcare facilities.

Both solutions align strategically with Indonesia's national health promotion policies, facilitating the country's healthcare digital transformation objectives.

Strategic Global Expansion Accelerates

Beyond the Indonesian contract, Inviz has secured additional international partnerships demonstrating strong global market reception:

  • Letter of Intent (LOI) worth approximately $1.2 million annually for AI Echo Care, an automated cardiac ultrasound analysis system
  • Memoranda of Understanding (MOUs) with companies in Singapore and Vietnam
  • Active discussions with healthcare institutions in Saudi Arabia and other Middle Eastern countries.

Slamet Riyanto, CEO of Telenasindo, commented: "Inviz's solutions provide the ideal answer for building Indonesia's digital healthcare infrastructure. Depending on market response, we are considering additional adoption of other products, including the AI Echo Care automated cardiac ultrasound system."

Proven Technology with Clinical Impact

"Inviz's AI healthcare platform delivers fast image loading, intelligent data management, and real-time collaboration among medical professionals, supporting clinical decision-making and substantially improving treatment outcomes," CEO Park stated. "This exhibition provided an invaluable opportunity to network with global healthcare leaders and confirm market reception for our solutions."

The platform's technical capabilities include:

  • Rapid medical image processing and loading
  • AI-powered intelligent data management
  • Real-time collaboration tools for medical teams
  • Integrated quality assurance workflows
  • Cloud-native architecture enabling accessible deployment

About the Inter-Ministerial Cooperation-Based AI Expansion Project

The Inter-Ministerial Cooperation-Based AI Expansion Project, initiated by South Korea's Ministry of Science and ICT and NIPA in 2022, aims to develop AI-based public services that citizens can directly experience. The program facilitates collaboration between government ministries—which provide field demands and public data—and private AI companies—which develop optimized solutions through a demonstration → improvement → practical application process.

The initiative spans critical areas including employment, welfare, disaster response, environment, and culture, with expectations for enhanced administrative efficiency, increased public safety, and improved welfare quality. The government plans to expand this program, transitioning it into the "Public AX (AI Transformation) Project" format to be pursued over the next six years, integrating private sector hyperscale AI technologies into public services and establishing a framework to verify tangible citizen benefits through demonstration and nationwide expansion.

About Inviz Inc.

 INVIZ Co., Ltd., led by CEO Sung-Chul Park, is a South Korean AI healthcare company specializing in cloud-based medical imaging platforms and digital healthcare solutions. The company's technologies encompass medical image quality assurance, AI-powered diagnostic support systems, and integrated healthcare management platforms. Inviz participates in multiple government-supported initiatives aimed at advancing Korea's AI healthcare capabilities and expanding into international markets.

Inviz's participation at GITEX GLOBAL 2025 was supported by NIPA's global expansion program for outstanding Korean ICT companies, reflecting the organization's commitment to facilitating international market entry for innovative Korean technology firms.
